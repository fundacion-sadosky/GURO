# Code of Conduct

## Purpose and values

The GURO project is a space open to teachers, students, designers and developers. We are committed to making participation in the project a harassment- and discrimination-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, ethnicity, religion or level of technical experience.

Since the project is used in educational contexts and may involve minors, the active protection of children and adolescents is a priority in all project spaces.

---

## Expected behavior

- Treat all people with respect and consideration.
- Use inclusive language appropriate for audiences of all ages.
- Accept constructive criticism with an open mind.
- Focus on what is best for the community and for the students who will use the project.
- Show empathy toward other community members.
- Respect the privacy of all people, especially minors.

---

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable in all project spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking or threats of any kind.
- Offensive or discriminatory comments related to personal characteristics.
- Publishing private information about other people without their explicit consent (doxing).
- Images, videos or text of a sexual nature or inappropriate for minors.
- Unsolicited or unsupervised direct contact with minors.
- Any conduct that may endanger the safety or well-being of minors.
- Sustained disruptions of conversations or events.
- Violation of third-party licenses.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all GURO project spaces:

- Repository: code, issues, Pull Requests, comments and reviews.
- Discussions on platforms associated with the project.
- In-person or virtual events related to the project.
- Public communications on behalf of the project.

---

## Consequences

The people responsible for maintaining the project may take the following actions in response to behaviors that violate this Code of Conduct:

1. **Warning:** private communication explaining the problematic behavior and what is expected going forward.
2. **Temporary moderation:** temporary restriction of participation in project spaces.
3. **Removal:** permanent exclusion from all project spaces in cases of serious, repeated conduct, or conduct that endangers the safety of minors.

Situations involving risk or harm to minors will be treated with the highest priority and may result in immediate removal without prior warning.

---

## How to report

If you witness or experience conduct that violates this Code of Conduct, you can report it by writing to:

info@fundacionsadosky.org.ar

Reports will be treated confidentially. Describe what happened, when and where, and include any context you consider relevant.
